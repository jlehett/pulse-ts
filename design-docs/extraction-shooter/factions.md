# Factions

Seven factions operate around the Zones. Each has a coherent motivation, a unique mechanical angle through its upgrade tree, a contract system, and a seasonal priority contract chain that advances its storyline.

Players can take contracts from any faction. Faction standing is earned through contract completion and spent on the faction's upgrade tree. Players can invest in multiple factions but can't max everything, creating meaningful build identity.

## Contract Structure

Each faction offers three tiers of contracts per raid:

### Standard Contracts

Available every raid. Generic objectives aligned with the faction's identity. "Extract 3 materials from Sector B" (Helix), "Scan 4 PvE entities" (UNZRA), "Disable the processing node" (Wardens). These build faction standing and provide faction currency for the upgrade tree.

### Specialist Contracts

Unlocked at higher faction standing. More complex, multi-objective missions. "Extract a specific rare material AND exfil through a specific point" (Vaunt), "Deploy sensors in 3 PoIs without being detected" (Edda). Better rewards, harder to complete.

### Priority Contracts

The seasonal story chain. One per season per faction, multi-raid quest lines (5–8 raids per chain) that advance the faction's narrative arc. Each raid in the chain has a specific objective in a specific Zone, and completing the chain unlocks a major story revelation, a unique reward (often a shell core — see [Shells](./shells.md)), and the next season's chain.

Priority contracts are the primary vehicle for lore delivery and narrative progression.

## Upgrade Trees

Each faction's tree has **3 branches** of ~8–10 nodes each, unlocked with faction currency earned from contracts. The three branches follow a consistent structure:

| Branch | Focus | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Operational** | Direct gameplay bonuses | Movement, combat, extraction advantages |
| **Technical** | Gear and economy bonuses | Stabilization, loot, crafting advantages |
| **Intelligence** | Information and awareness | Map intel, event prediction, PvE behavior insight |

Each faction's branches are flavored differently. UNZRA's Operational branch is about scanning and analysis in the field. The Wardens' Operational branch is about damage and disruption. Same structural slot, completely different identity.

## Faction Dynamics

| Conflict | Nature |
|---|---|
| **Helix vs. Wardens** | Helix profits from Zone stability; Wardens want to destroy them. Direct economic conflict. |
| **UNZRA vs. Meridian** | UNZRA wants cautious study; Meridian wants acceleration. Philosophical conflict. |
| **Wardens vs. Meridian** | Diametrically opposed. Destruction vs. completion. The game's central ideological axis. |
| **Helix vs. UNZRA** | Helix resists regulation and data sharing. UNZRA prevents corporate monopolies. Political conflict. |
| **Vaunt vs. Everyone** | Vaunt operates in the cracks between factions, selling to whoever pays. Tolerated but not trusted. |
| **Edda vs. Wardens** | Edda wants to preserve and study; Wardens want to destroy. Wardens have destroyed irreplaceable data. |
| **RECLAM vs. Meridian** | Both want long-term Zone presence, but RECLAM wants human control and Meridian wants symbiosis. |

## Helix Solutions

**Type:** Private military contractor / corporate extraction firm

**Motivation:** Profit. Helix secures Zone materials for commercial clients. They don't care what the Architect is building as long as the Zones keep producing valuable materials.

**Runner relationship:** Employer. Most freelance Runners take Helix contracts. Good pay, minimal oversight, no ideological baggage.

**Stance on the relay:** Keep it running. The Zones are the most profitable thing in human history. Don't mess with the revenue stream.

**Upgrade tree focus:** Loot and economy. Better stabilization efficiency (less material cost per gear), expanded extraction capacity (carry more out), market access (better sell prices, exclusive trade options). Helix Runners bring more home.

**Priority contract arc:** Helix is mapping the commercial potential of every Zone. Their seasonal storyline follows their attempts to *industrialize* extraction — building forward operating bases near Zone boundaries, automating material processing, and dealing with the consequences when the Architect's response systems start treating their infrastructure as material to reclaim.

**Narrative role:** The pragmatic voice. They're not evil — they provide jobs, stabilize the economy, fund research (when it's profitable). But they'll suppress information that threatens their bottom line, and they've quietly sabotaged efforts to interfere with Zone operations.

## UNZRA (United Nations Zone Research Authority)

**Type:** International governmental body

**Motivation:** Understanding and containment. UNZRA exists to study the Zones, share findings internationally, and prevent any single nation or corporation from monopolizing Zone access.

**Runner relationship:** Employer (research-focused). UNZRA contracts pay less but give access to research data, better stabilization tech, and institutional support.

**Stance on the relay:** Understand before acting. UNZRA wants complete knowledge of the relay's function before anyone touches it. They advocate for caution and multilateral decision-making.

**Upgrade tree focus:** Scanning and intel. Better detection of Zone processes, ability to identify PvE entity function before engaging, data fragment analysis tools (more lore from each fragment found), map reveal capabilities. UNZRA Runners know more about what's around them.

**Priority contract arc:** UNZRA is building a unified model of how all Zones interconnect. Their seasonal storyline follows researchers closing in on the relay's purpose — each season, a new connection is mapped, a new function is decoded. But UNZRA's findings keep getting leaked to other factions, and the political question of *who controls the research* threatens to tear the organization apart.

**Narrative role:** The institutional voice. Well-intentioned but slow, political, and compromised by member nations' competing interests. They have the best researchers but the worst bureaucracy.

## The Wardens

**Type:** Militant containment organization

**Motivation:** Destroy the Zones. The Wardens believe the relay represents an existential threat and that completion must be prevented at any cost.

**Runner relationship:** Contractor. Wardens send Runners in to *sabotage* — disrupt Zone processes, damage infrastructure, extract materials specifically to cause defects in the Architect's construction.

**Stance on the relay:** Destroy it. Every Zone is a component, and every component damaged is progress toward safety.

**Upgrade tree focus:** Combat and disruption. Bonus damage to PvE entities, ability to disable Zone processes (temporarily shut down a node's function for tactical advantage), hardened gear (more resistant to Zone environmental effects), extraction denial tools. Wardens are the combat specialists.

**Priority contract arc:** The Wardens are escalating — from sabotaging individual Zone processes to attempting to destroy entire Zone components. Their seasonal storyline follows increasingly ambitious operations and the blowback they cause. A Warden operation damages a Synthesis Zone's primary process, and the Architect *reroutes production to a different Zone* — the first evidence that destroying one Zone doesn't stop the relay, it just changes the construction plan. This forces the Wardens to confront whether their strategy can ever work.

**Narrative role:** The fear voice. They're not wrong that the relay could be catastrophically dangerous. But their methods are extreme, and sabotaging Zone infrastructure has unpredictable consequences. A Warden operation in a Synthesis Zone once caused a material cascade that killed twelve Runners and expanded the Zone boundary by 200 meters — the only known Zone expansion event.

**Dark thread:** Some Wardens have access to a classified UNZRA report suggesting the relay might be *repurposed* — turned from a Network node into something humanity controls. The Wardens suppress this because it contradicts their "destroy everything" mandate.

## Meridian

**Type:** Transhumanist research collective

**Motivation:** Accelerate the relay's completion. Meridian believes the Network represents contact with a vastly superior intelligence and that connection to it is the most important event in human history.

**Runner relationship:** Contractor (research-focused). Meridian Runners are sent to *assist* Zone processes — deliver materials where the Architect needs them, repair damage caused by other Runners, study and facilitate the relay's construction.

**Stance on the relay:** Complete it. Help the Architect finish. Open the door.

**Upgrade tree focus:** Zone adaptation. Reduced PvE aggro (entities are less likely to target you if you're not disrupting processes), ability to interact with Zone systems (activate dormant processes, redirect material flows for beneficial effects), Imprint tolerance (operate in deeper, more altered areas). Meridian Runners go deeper and come back changed.

**Priority contract arc:** Meridian is trying to communicate with the Architect — not through language, but through action. By assisting Zone processes in specific sequences, they're attempting to demonstrate intentionality to an intelligence that doesn't recognize them as significant. Their seasonal storyline follows early failures, incremental pattern-matching, and the first terrifying moment when the Architect *responds* — not with communication, but by adjusting a process in a way that only makes sense if it's accounting for Meridian's assistance.

**Narrative role:** The aspiration voice. Meridian researchers have made genuine breakthroughs by studying Zone processes cooperatively rather than extractively. Their science is good. But their willingness to help an alien construction project complete itself — without knowing what comes through the door — is either visionary or suicidal.

**Dark thread:** Meridian's founder was inside a Zone during its formation. She should be dead. She isn't. She won't explain what happened. Her understanding of Zone processes is decades ahead of anyone else's. See [Lore Overview — Meridian's Founder](./lore-overview.md#meridians-founder).

## Edda

**Type:** Archivists, historians, and cartographers

**Motivation:** Document everything. Edda believes that understanding the Zones — their pre-Imprint history, their current state, their patterns — is humanity's most important task. Not to control them or destroy them, but to *know* them.

**Runner relationship:** Employer (exploration-focused). Edda contracts pay in information as much as currency: research data, map intel, decoded fragments.

**Stance on the relay:** Study it completely before anyone acts. Edda is philosophically aligned with UNZRA but frustrated by UNZRA's political compromises. Edda publishes findings openly; UNZRA classifies them.

**Upgrade tree focus:** Exploration and discovery. Expanded fog-of-war reveal, ability to find hidden nodes (rooms, passages, caches that other factions can't detect), enhanced data fragment drops, map event prediction (advance warning of events from the Zone's event pool). Edda Runners find things nobody else does.

**Priority contract arc:** Edda is cataloguing the Architect's full construction plan by cross-referencing Zone layouts across the world. Their seasonal storyline is the most lore-heavy — each season, Edda pieces together another section of the relay's blueprint. They're the faction that discovers the cross-Zone connections in Era 2, the relay's purpose in Era 3, and the existence of the other relay node at 4.3 light years. But Edda has a problem: they hoard information. They claim to publish openly, but their founder has been collecting anomalous items that don't fit any theory — items that suggest the Architect's construction plan has *changed* since it started.

**Narrative role:** The knowledge voice. Edda provides the most direct lore access. Their priority contracts are where the big revelations happen. But their neutrality is a performance — they have a perspective, and their selective publishing shapes the narrative.

## Vaunt

**Type:** Black market network and high-risk extraction syndicate

**Motivation:** Opportunity. Vaunt operates in the spaces between factions — smuggling materials, running unauthorized operations in contested Zones, and selling to whoever pays the most.

**Runner relationship:** Partners, not employees. Vaunt doesn't hire Runners — it offers *deals*. Higher-risk, higher-reward contracts that other factions won't sanction.

**Stance on the relay:** Whatever's most profitable. Vaunt has no ideological position and will sell to Wardens and Meridian alike.

**Upgrade tree focus:** Risk and reward amplification. Access to unstable Zone materials (higher value but dangerous to carry — if you die they damage your other cards), PvP-focused tools (better tracking of other Runners, ambush capabilities), black market access (sell to buyers who pay more for materials other factions won't touch). Vaunt Runners gamble big.

**Priority contract arc:** Vaunt's seasonal storyline follows their discovery that certain Zone materials are being bought by entities that don't appear to be human, corporate, or governmental. Someone — or something — outside the known factions is accumulating Imprint artifacts. Who are they? What are they building? Vaunt doesn't care about the answer on principle, but they care very much about who's spending that much money.

**Narrative role:** The underworld voice. Vaunt sees things other factions miss because they operate without restrictions. Their contracts take you to places and situations other factions won't touch. The moral gray zone — Vaunt isn't evil, but they don't ask questions about who they're selling to, and that has consequences.

## RECLAM (Reclamation Engineering Corps)

**Type:** Military engineering organization

**Motivation:** Reclaim Zone territory. RECLAM's mission is establishing permanent human presence inside Zone boundaries — pushing the Architect back, holding ground, building forward.

**Runner relationship:** Employer (engineering-focused). RECLAM contracts involve deployment, construction, and territory assessment alongside extraction.

**Stance on the relay:** Control it. RECLAM doesn't want to destroy the Zones or help them complete — they want to establish human sovereignty over Zone territory. If the relay can be seized and repurposed, RECLAM wants to be the ones holding it.

**Upgrade tree focus:** Fortification and support. Ability to deploy temporary structures on nodes (barricades, signal jammers, supply caches that persist for the raid), team-oriented upgrades (share intel with allies, provide cover bonuses to nearby friendly Runners), node control mechanics. RECLAM Runners shape the battlefield.

**Priority contract arc:** RECLAM's seasonal storyline follows the engineering challenge of building inside the Zones. Structures get Imprinted. Materials degrade. But RECLAM starts finding ways to stabilize small areas — pockets of "clean" space inside the Zones. The implication: if you can stabilize a pocket, you can *live* inside a Zone. RECLAM is building toward permanent human habitation in Zone territory, which puts them on a collision course with the Wardens (who want Zones destroyed, not colonized) and Meridian (who see human habitation as contaminating the Architect's work).

**Narrative role:** The sovereignty voice. RECLAM is the military-industrial answer to the Zones: if we can't destroy them, we take them. They built the extraction infrastructure that every Runner uses. They maintain the boundary cordons. They're the reason civilians can live near Zones safely. But their ambition to colonize Zone territory raises the question of what happens when humans try to live alongside the Architect's construction.

**Dark thread:** RECLAM engineers working deep inside Zones have started reporting that their stabilized structures are developing properties they didn't design. The "clean" pockets aren't fully clean — they're *differently Imprinted*. RECLAM hasn't published this.

## Independents (Freelance Runners)

Independents are not a faction — they're the absence of one. The player's default state.

No faction baggage, no agenda. Independents take contracts from whoever's paying and answer to nobody. Most Runners start independent and develop faction relationships over time.

Independents can take standard contracts from any faction without standing requirements. Specialist and priority contracts require faction standing.

The independent experience is the baseline: you enter the Zone, you loot, you extract, you survive. The factions provide context, narrative, and progression layers on top of that baseline.
